Max Lenail

Hey there,

I'm Max Lenail, one of the photographers in the Xenophos group.

I am currently a student at Palo Alto High School, where I play soccer and ultimate frisbee, as well as do debate. However, my adventure in light-painting started when I was in Middle school, where I was lucky enough to join a group of awesome people with an awesome idea.

I've always loved photography. When I was younger, it was an exciting activity to do on vacations, where I would use Kodak disposable cameras to capture anything I found cool or interesting. When I got my first digital camera, I really got into it, and started to not only take photos during trips, but also around the house, or in my neighborhood. It was after my trip to Nicaragua, when I got my first DSLR, that I began to think of photography in a different way, as more than just a convenient way to immortalize memories. I started taking pictures for their artistic value, as opposed to just what was in the frame.

When my brother, Alex, showed me an online tutorial on how to do basic light-painting, I was thrilled. I thought it would be a great way to learn all the features of my camera, and I was right. We started fooling around with glow-sticks and flashlights, and soon discovered that we had found something really special. Alex got a group of his friends together, built some awesome tools, and the rest is in our photos. In the end, I am so grateful and happy that I was a part of this group, as I wouldn't be half the photographer I am today without it.

Anna McGarrigle

Hi,

I'm Anna.

Art became a chief interest of mine after my father left me in my room with a box of acrylic paints and found me hours later surrounded by four walls covered in mini-murals dictated by my overzealous imagination. Since then, my medium has narrowed to digital art; designing, photographing and writing for an assortment of newspapers and magazines has sharpened my interests. Over the past two years, working with the Xenophos crew has greatly developed not only my passion but also my experience as a photographer. Before Xenophos, I never would have considered photography to be a group effort, but our productive teamwork and friendship show how working together allows us to develop from each other.

Here at the George Washington University, I study Anthropology and Fine Arts. I also write and design for the GW Hatchet, bringing my current studies inside and outside of the classroom back to art.

Masha Andreyeva

Hi,

I'm Masha.

I’ve been visually inclined since I was five years old, when I was better at understanding Art Nouveau books than I was at English. In 8th grade I picked up my mom’s Konica Minolta with all of its endless buttons, and thus began my adventures with SLRs. I’ve dabbled into darkroom photography, Photoshop, and a variety of exposure techniques, also growing a parallel affinity for design, as I created little projects in Illustrator and worked on putting together an arts magazine. Somewhere in there at an art show, I met Alex and Bradford who drew me in to be fascinated with light manipulation and all of the ideas, laughs, and frozen yogurt trips that came along with it. My work with Xenophos brought me delight, skill, and direction to my current project—college, that is. I now study Cognitive Science and Design at UC Berkeley, with a focus in human-centered design and innovation.
